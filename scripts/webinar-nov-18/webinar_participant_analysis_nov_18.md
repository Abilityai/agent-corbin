## Recommended Presentation Adjustments

### 1. **Strengthen the Implementation Focus (Most Critical)**

**Current Plan:** Good coverage, but can go deeper.

**Recommended Changes:**
- **Add a "Half-Baked Implementation" section** addressing Melissa's pain point directly
- **Show implementation timeline** for each case study (how long did it take?)
- **Include "Red Flags"** - what signals indicate you're heading for failure
- **Practical first steps** - what to do Monday morning after the webinar

### 2. **Address Information Overload/Hype Explicitly**

**Current Plan:** Anti-hype messaging is there, but could be more explicit.

**Recommended Changes:**
- **Open with Konstantin's quote:** "Lack of time to digest all of the hype and content flowing all around"
- **Create a "Cutting Through the Noise" slide** - what to ignore vs. what matters
- **Tool evaluation framework** - simple criteria for evaluating AI tools/vendors
- **One-pager checklist** they can use to evaluate any AI opportunity

### 3. **Emphasize the Adoption Gap More Heavily**

**Current Plan:** Covered in the 3 gaps section.

**Recommended Changes:**
- **Make adoption a recurring theme** throughout all case studies
- **Show specific adoption tactics** you used (how did you get teams to use it?)
- **Address "cultural change"** explicitly (Benjamin's challenge)
- **Include "how to frame this to your team"** in the 90-day plan

### 4. **Add Measurement & ROI Tracking**

**Current Plan:** Strong ROI numbers, but light on "how to measure."

**Recommended Changes:**
- **Add "How We Measured This" section** to each case study
- **Provide a simple ROI calculation template** they can use
- **Include leading indicators** - what to track before revenue impact shows
- **Address "proving value to executives"** for middle managers in audience

### 5. **Strengthen the "Getting Started" Pathway**

**Current Plan:** 90-day plan is good, but can be more concrete.

**Recommended Changes:**
- **Week 1 specific actions** - exactly what to do in the first week
- **Decision tree:** "If you have X challenge, start with Y approach"
- **Small team version** - what if you don't have offshore developers (Ulf's concern)
- **"Start tomorrow" checklist** - no-budget actions they can take immediately

### 6. **Address Resource Constraints**

**Current Plan:** Implicit in case studies, but not explicit.

**Recommended Changes:**
- **Show team size** for each case study - "I built this myself" or "2-person team"
- **Address Ulf's concern** - you don't need massive dev teams
- **Budget ranges** - what's realistic for different company sizes
- **"AI as talent multiplier"** - Dmitry's "fill missing gaps" angle

## Final Recommendations

### DO:
✅ **Reference participant challenges directly** - show you heard them
✅ **Keep it practical over theoretical** - this audience wants "how," not "why"
✅ **Show implementation timelines** - they need to know it's achievable
✅ **Provide take-home frameworks** - checklist, decision tree, evaluation criteria
✅ **Address "half-baked" explicitly** - this is a major pain point
✅ **Emphasize you built these yourself** - small teams can do this

### DON'T:
❌ **Don't assume technical knowledge** - some are very new to AI
❌ **Don't skip the "getting started" steps** - they need concrete first actions
❌ **Don't oversell** - this audience is skeptical of hype
❌ **Don't skip measurement** - ROI matters to this group
❌ **Don't ignore organizational challenges** - adoption is a real concern
