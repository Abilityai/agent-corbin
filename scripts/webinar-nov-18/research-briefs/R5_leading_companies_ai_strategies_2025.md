# Research Brief R5: What Leading Companies Are Doing Differently with AI (2025)

## Research Objective
Identify specific strategies and approaches that separate AI leaders from AI laggards in 2025. What do the "15% who succeed" do differently from everyone else?

## Target Timeframe
**2025 data only** - strategies documented or reported in 2025

## Key Research Questions

1. **Who are the recognized AI leaders in 2025?**
   - Which companies are cited as AI success stories?
   - By industry, who's leading?
   - What makes them leaders (vs. just early adopters)?

2. **What do AI leaders do differently in the VISION/STRATEGY phase?**
   - How do they approach AI strategy?
   - How do they select use cases?
   - How do they align AI with business strategy?
   - Decision-making frameworks they use
   - How do they avoid "random acts of AI"?

3. **What do AI leaders do differently in the FOUNDATION phase?**
   - How do they approach data readiness?
   - Tech stack and architecture decisions
   - Integration strategies
   - How do they avoid "garbage in, garbage out"?

4. **What do AI leaders do differently in the ADOPTION phase?**
   - Change management approaches
   - Training and enablement
   - User experience focus
   - How do they drive actual usage vs. resistance?

5. **What organizational structures do AI leaders use?**
   - Centralized vs. decentralized AI teams
   - Centers of excellence
   - Cross-functional collaboration models
   - Roles: Chief AI Officer, AI Product Managers, AI Ethics roles

6. **What talent strategies do AI leaders employ?**
   - Build vs. buy vs. partner for AI talent
   - Training and upskilling approaches
   - How do they attract and retain AI talent?
   - External partnerships and consulting relationships

7. **How do AI leaders approach build vs. buy decisions?**
   - When do they build custom vs. adopt platforms?
   - Vendor selection criteria
   - Partnership strategies
   - Avoiding vendor lock-in

8. **What experimentation and learning approaches do AI leaders use?**
   - Pilot methodologies
   - Fail-fast cultures
   - Learning from failures
   - Scaling successful pilots

9. **How do AI leaders measure and communicate success?**
   - Metrics and KPIs
   - Reporting to board and executives
   - Internal communication
   - External communication (earnings calls, press)

10. **What common patterns separate leaders from laggards?**
    - Strategic patterns
    - Organizational patterns
    - Cultural patterns
    - Process patterns

## Priority Data Sources

**High Priority:**
- McKinsey AI leaders research 2025
- BCG AI maturity benchmarking 2025
- MIT Sloan AI adoption research
- Harvard Business Review AI leadership case studies
- Gartner AI maturity models and leader profiles
- Fortune / Forbes AI leaders profiles
- Fast Company most innovative companies (AI category)

**Company-Specific:**
- Earnings call transcripts (AI discussions)
- CEO/CTO interviews and profiles
- Public statements on AI strategy
- Technology blogs and engineering posts

**Industry Analyst:**
- Forrester Wave reports (AI platforms, AI services)
- Gartner Magic Quadrant (AI-related categories)
- IDC AI maturity frameworks

**Avoid:**
- Vendor case studies (biased)
- Self-proclaimed "AI leaders" without third-party validation
- Hype pieces without substance
- Pre-2024 AI leadership examples (may not translate to GenAI era)

## Deliverable Format

Produce a markdown document with:

1. **Executive Summary** (key findings)
   - What separates AI leaders from everyone else
   - Top 3-5 differentiating factors
   - Strategic implications

2. **AI Leaders Identified** (by industry/category)
   - Who's recognized as leading in 2025
   - What they're known for
   - Validation sources

3. **Leadership Patterns by "Gap"**
   - **Vision Gap:** How leaders do strategy differently
   - **Foundation Gap:** How leaders build data/tech foundation differently
   - **Adoption Gap:** How leaders drive usage differently
   - Specific practices, examples, contrasts with laggards

4. **Organizational Structures and Roles**
   - How AI leaders organize for AI
   - Key roles and responsibilities
   - Centralized vs. decentralized models

5. **Talent Strategies**
   - How leaders acquire and develop AI talent
   - Build vs. buy vs. partner approaches
   - Training and enablement programs

6. **Build vs. Buy Decision Patterns**
   - When leaders build custom vs. adopt platforms
   - Vendor selection and partnership strategies
   - Examples and decision criteria

7. **Experimentation and Learning Approaches**
   - Pilot methodologies
   - Fail-fast and learning culture
   - Scaling strategies

8. **Measurement and Communication**
   - How leaders measure AI success
   - How they report to boards and executives
   - How they communicate externally

9. **Common Success Patterns** (cross-cutting themes)
   - Strategic patterns
   - Organizational patterns
   - Cultural patterns
   - Process patterns

10. **Contrast with Laggards** (what NOT to do)
    - Where laggards go wrong
    - Anti-patterns to avoid

11. **Strategic Implications for Webinar**
    - How to position "what the 15% do differently"
    - Which leadership practices to highlight
    - Credible examples to reference

12. **Source List** (all sources with dates and links)

## Why This Research Matters for the Webinar

**For High-Value Participants:**
- **CEO Coaches** need to guide clients toward leadership practices (not just competency)
- **VCs** need to identify which portfolio companies have leadership potential
- **Enterprise Leaders** need to benchmark against actual leaders, not theory

**For the Presentation:**
- Provides concrete examples of "what the 15% do differently"
- Validates the "3 gaps" framework with leader practices
- Shows the path from where participants are to where they want to be
- Builds aspiration: "Here's what great looks like"

**Supports Core Positioning:**
- "85% fail - but here's what the 15% do differently"
- Grounds success patterns in actual companies
- Provides case examples beyond just AbilityAI

## Success Criteria

Research is successful if it provides:
- ✅ 5-10 recognized AI leaders in 2025 (validated by third parties)
- ✅ 3-5 key differentiators between leaders and laggards
- ✅ Specific practices leaders employ for Vision, Foundation, Adoption gaps
- ✅ Organizational and talent patterns of leaders
- ✅ Build vs. buy patterns and examples
- ✅ Can credibly say "Here's what companies like [X] do that others don't"
- ✅ Anti-patterns and what NOT to do (from laggard analysis)
