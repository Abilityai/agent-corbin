# Research Brief R8: AI Adoption and Change Management (2025)

## Research Objective
Understand how companies are successfully driving AI adoption in 2025 - not just deploying technology, but getting teams to actually use it. Address the "Adoption Gap" with concrete strategies and examples.

## Target Timeframe
**2025 data only** - adoption challenges and solutions documented in 2025

## Key Research Questions

1. **What are the main AI adoption challenges companies face in 2025?**
   - Ranked by frequency and severity
   - User resistance and reasons
   - Cultural barriers
   - Skill gaps and training needs
   - Change fatigue
   - Trust and transparency issues

2. **What's the current state of AI adoption rates in 2025?**
   - What percentage of deployed AI tools are actually being used?
   - Usage rates by use case type
   - Adoption drop-off patterns (initial vs. sustained use)
   - Difference between "deployed" and "adopted"

3. **What change management approaches are working for AI adoption?**
   - Stakeholder engagement strategies
   - Communication and messaging approaches
   - Training and enablement programs
   - Incentive and accountability structures
   - Leadership sponsorship models

4. **What role does user experience play in AI adoption?**
   - How UX design impacts adoption rates
   - Examples of high-adoption AI tools (what makes them work)
   - Examples of low-adoption AI tools (what went wrong)
   - "Built for users" vs. "built for engineers"

5. **How are companies addressing AI resistance and fear?**
   - Job displacement fears
   - Loss of control concerns
   - "AI will replace me" messaging
   - Trust and transparency strategies
   - Building AI literacy and confidence

6. **What training and enablement approaches work for AI?**
   - Training models (formal, informal, peer-to-peer)
   - Enablement resources (documentation, champions, support)
   - Time to proficiency
   - Ongoing learning and skill development

7. **What's the role of leadership in AI adoption?**
   - CEO and C-suite sponsorship impact
   - Manager role in driving adoption
   - Champions and ambassadors
   - Leading by example (executives using AI)

8. **How do adoption patterns vary by use case and audience?**
   - Customer-facing AI vs. internal AI
   - Knowledge workers vs. operational workers
   - Sales teams vs. service teams vs. technical teams
   - Early adopters vs. late majority

9. **What metrics do companies use to measure AI adoption?**
   - Usage metrics (active users, frequency, depth)
   - Value realization metrics (outcomes from usage)
   - Sentiment and satisfaction metrics
   - How to diagnose adoption problems

10. **What are the "cultural change" strategies for AI?**
    - Participant concern: "Cultural change" (Benjamin Rey-Ilini)
    - How to shift culture to embrace AI
    - Building AI-positive cultures
    - Overcoming change resistance

11. **How do leading companies "frame AI initiatives and AI adoption"?**
    - Participant concern: "Framing AI initiatives and AI adoption" (Astrid Gommeaux)
    - Internal messaging strategies
    - Value proposition framing
    - How to position AI as empowerment vs. replacement

## Why This Research Matters for the Webinar

**For High-Value Participants:**
- **CEO Coaches** need to help clients drive AI adoption across organizations
- **VCs** need to ensure portfolio companies achieve usage, not just deployment
- **Enterprise Leaders** need change management strategies for AI rollouts
- **Consultants** need adoption playbooks for client work

**For the Presentation:**
- Validates "Adoption Gap" as a critical failure point
- Provides concrete strategies beyond just "build user-friendly tools"
- Addresses cultural and organizational dimensions of AI success
- Shows you understand people challenges, not just technology

**Addresses Participant Concerns:**
- "Cultural change" (Benjamin Rey-Ilini) - direct research focus
- "Framing AI initiatives and AI adoption" (Astrid Gommeaux) - messaging strategies
- "Half-baked implementation ends up hurting the team" (Melissa Huertas) - adoption failures
- "Introduce benefits to my team" (Iva Marinova) - internal change management

**Strategic Positioning:**
- You build tools teams actually use (CEO Content Engine example)
- Adoption by design, not afterthought
- User experience and organizational change expertise
